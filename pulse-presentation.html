The solution — how we solve it

Stop sending nudges. Run journeys.

1
Sense. Four specialist AI agents investigate every customer in parallel — spending, cash-flow, risk & consent, product-fit — each with its own tools.
2
Decide. An orchestrator agent weighs the four reports and rules: engage with care, engage for growth — or stay silent.
3
Engage. A conversation agent executes in the customer's language, adapting to every reply — and takes real banking actions on agreement.
4
Restrain. On detected financial stress: all cross-sell suppressed, help first. Trust is the product.
5
Learn. Every outcome trains the policy — the system gets measurably better with use.

⚖️ Why this split matters
The model can never bypass consent, contact windows, or caps — those live in plain, reviewable code. This is the 2026 best practice for agentic banking (“architecture before agent”).
The AI core — three tiers of agents, all real

No scripts. No if/else. The models decide.

1 · Specialist analysts
Four LLM agents with distinct roles and restricted tool access investigate in parallel. The cash-flow analyst reported “model projection is unreliable” on its own — it read the forecaster's holdout skill.
2 · Orchestrator
Synthesizes the four reports and decides — including “stay silent” (Rohan: “financially healthy, no intervention required”) and consent-aware restraint (Neha, unprompted).
3 · Conversation agent
A ReAct tool-calling loop: free-text chat in Hindi, Odia or English, and real actions — “set a ₹15,000 cap” → it calls the banking API, sets it, confirms.
+ Trained forecaster
Per-customer cash-flow model, validated on a 30-day holdout (skill up to 94% vs naive — negatives shown too). Predicted Priya's low balance 9 days ahead.
Lightweight production LLM · structured JSON + function calling · model-agnostic (swappable to an India-hosted / Banking-BHASHINI model) · rule fallback exists for outages and is labelled “no AI” in the UI.

The moat

The demo is reproducible.
The flywheel isn't.

1
Outcome feedback flywheel. A Thompson-sampling policy learns which tone works per segment from real outcomes — live journeys apply the learned tone and feed results back. Using the product trains it.
2
Verified learning. From ≈random start to +12.9pp over random, 91% of the theoretical ceiling — and it correctly discovers the hidden best tone for every segment.
3
Only compounds at SBI's scale. Converging the policy needs a stream of consented outcomes only a ~100M-customer base produces. A competitor can clone the UI; they can't clone the data flywheel.
4
Compliance lock-in. Consent, TRAI, audit and human-in-loop wired in from day one — the months-long certification work that blocks every would-be replacement.

Roadmap — demo to platform

Phased, measurable, de-risked

Phase 0 · now
Working MVP. Live multi-agent mesh, ReAct banking actions, trained forecaster, guardrails, vernacular, audit trail, uplift measurement, flywheel.
Phase 1 · 1–3 mo
Pilot. Consented Account Aggregator + YONO event streams; real holdout uplift on a volunteer cohort; human-in-loop on all high-value.
Phase 2 · 3–9 mo
Rollout. Push/IVR channels, 10–15 journeys, gamification tie-in with YONO 2.0, Bhashini vernacular, DLT registration.
Phase 3 · 9–24 mo
Platform. Journey Studio for business users, RM copilot, 22 languages, edge/offline, model governance & drift monitoring.
Each phase gates on its own KPIs — engagement lift vs holdout, dormant-revival rate, opt-out rate (low = healthy), products per customer.
